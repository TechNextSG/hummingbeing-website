What Is Somatic Bodywork?

Working Through the Body,
Not On It

Somatic Bodywork is a holistic coaching approach that works through the body rather than on it. The word "soma" has deep roots: in ancient Greek it means "the living body in its wholeness", while in Sanskrit it evokes "the flow of consciousness."

This practice helps you reconnect with yourself — your deepest self — and develop ways of being that align with your current goals and needs by tapping into the wisdom that lives in the body.

Using the Strozzi Somatic Bodywork method, Isabelle employs a gentle, systematic process to help clients release historical patterns, old contractions and held tension. As the body opens and releases, individuals gain access to increased energy and aliveness — which expands their capacity for choice and enables them to respond differently to life's circumstances.

The Methodology

The Strozzi Somatic Bodywork Approach

The Strozzi Institute, founded by Richard Strozzi-Heckler, has developed one of the most respected somatic bodywork methodologies in the world — used by leaders, athletes, therapists and coaches globally.

The approach is built on a simple but profound insight: the body holds the shape of our history. Every significant experience, every adapted response to stress and trauma, leaves a physical imprint — a contraction, a posture, a chronic holding pattern.

Strozzi Somatic Bodywork uses specific touch, movement, breath and inquiry to address these physical imprints directly — releasing them gently and systematically, and creating the conditions for new, more resourceful ways of being.

  • Gentle, targeted touch to identify and release holding patterns
  • Movement and breath work to support release and integration
  • Somatic inquiry to deepen awareness and insight
  • Props including gymnastic balls and elastic bands
  • Playful, exploratory sessions that respect your pace

In Session

What a Session Looks Like

Sessions are experiential, collaborative and led at your pace.

1

Arrival & Check-In

Sessions begin with a gentle check-in — noting what's present in your body, mind and emotional landscape. There is no agenda imposed.

2

Somatic Assessment

Isabelle observes and gently explores how your body is holding — identifying areas of tension, restriction or collapse that may be limiting your full expression.

3

Guided Release Work

Using touch, movement, breath, props and inquiry, specific holding patterns are invited to release. You remain fully clothed and in complete control throughout.

4

Integration & Closing

Each session closes with integration time — resting in the shifts, noticing what has changed, and taking new practices into the days ahead.

The Word Behind the Practice

Soma (σṖμα) — The Living Body in Its Wholeness

In ancient Greek, "soma" referred not just to the physical body but to the entirety of the living, experiencing organism. In Sanskrit, "soma" evoked the flow of consciousness through the body. Both traditions understood what modern neuroscience now confirms: the body and mind are inseparable — and real transformation must engage both.

Somatic Bodywork honours this ancient understanding while drawing on cutting-edge neuroscience — bridging Eastern wisdom and Western science in a practice that is both deeply human and rigorously evidence-based.
